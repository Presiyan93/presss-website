Food News

Plant-Based Seafood on the Rise: Whole Foods Market has highlighted a growing trend in plant-based seafood alternatives, such as faux tuna, crab cakes, and salmon substitutes. With ingredients like konjac root and trumpet mushrooms mimicking the texture of seafood, this trend caters to consumers seeking plant-based options or looking to reduce their seafood consumption.


Gut-Friendly Beverages Expanding: Recoup has launched prebiotic beverages nationwide at Sprouts Farmers Market, promoting the benefits of gut health and regenerative agriculture. These drinks aim to meet consumer demand for functional foods with environmental benefits


Noodle Innovations: Instant noodles are getting an upgrade, with brands introducing more gourmet options that go beyond the typical instant ramen. Economy News

U.S. Consumer Sentiment Slips: American consumers have become more pessimistic about the economy this month, with the University of Michigan's consumer sentiment index slipping to 68.9 from September's 70.1. High prices remain a significant concern despite some improvements in inflation. Factors such as the Federal Reserve’s recent interest rate cuts and ongoing global turmoil, including in the Middle East, are influencing consumer outlooks.


U.S. Trade Deficit: The U.S. trade deficit fell to $70.4 billion in August 2024, down from $78.9 billion in July. This improvement was driven by increased exports and a reduction in imports, particularly in the goods sector. The services sector also saw a slight increase in its trade surplus.


Personal Income and Spending: In August, U.S. personal income rose by 0.2%, with similar increases in consumer spending. Despite the rise, consumers remain cautious, and saving rates stayed at 4.8%.
